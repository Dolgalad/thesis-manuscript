Inference tasks
MAP inference
find one best assignment
\[ x^\star \in \arg\max_x p(x) \]
Marginal inference
estimate local beliefs
variable beliefs \(\mu_i(x_i)\), factor beliefs \(\mu_\alpha(x_\alpha)\)
\[ \mu_i(x_i) = p(X_i=x_i) = \sum_{x_{V\setminus\{i\}}} p(x) \]
Conditional marginal inference
recompute beliefs after fixing part of the assignment
Belief propagation
computes marginals exactly on trees and approximately on loopy graphs
This slide bridges inference tasks before introducing belief propagation. MAP inference searches for a single best assignment. Marginal inference estimates local beliefs over variables and factors. Conditional marginal inference recomputes those beliefs after part of the assignment has been fixed. Belief propagation is then introduced as the algorithmic mechanism for computing marginals exactly on trees and approximately on loopy graphs.
