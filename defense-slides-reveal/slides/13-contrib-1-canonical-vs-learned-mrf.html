The canonical overcomplete construction has already been introduced in the background. This slide should not re-derive it. The point is simply that the factor scopes stay fixed: they are induced by the BILP variable-constraint graph. What changes is the source of the parameters. In the canonical MRF, the parameters are fixed by a hand-designed mapping from the problem. In the learned MRF, the same types of parameter tables are predicted from GNN embeddings using variable and factor heads.
