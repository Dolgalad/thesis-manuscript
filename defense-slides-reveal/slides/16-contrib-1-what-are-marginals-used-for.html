This slide merges the previous marginal-usage slide and the decoding-strategy slide. The important message is that marginals are not independent classifier probabilities and they are not directly solutions. They are soft beliefs produced by inference in the MRF, so they already reflect neighboring constraints through message passing or mean-field updates. However, because they are soft local quantities, we still need a decoder. Entropy-guided greedy decoding can be mentioned as a simple baseline: it fixes variables according to confidence under the current marginals. The main method is guided decimation: after fixing some variables, condition the MRF on the partial assignment and rerun inference to obtain conditional marginals for the remaining variables.
