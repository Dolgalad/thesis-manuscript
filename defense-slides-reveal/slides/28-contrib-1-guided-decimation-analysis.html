The learned MRF assigns different log-potential regimes to feasible and infeasible local configurations.

This slide explains why guided decimation is different from one-shot scoring. On the left, the same free variable can become more or less attractive once a partial solution has been fixed and inference is rerun. On the right, the predicted theta values show that the learned MRF has learned to assign different log-potential regimes to feasible and infeasible local assignments. Together, this explains why recomputed structured marginals provide better decoding signals than independent variable scores.
