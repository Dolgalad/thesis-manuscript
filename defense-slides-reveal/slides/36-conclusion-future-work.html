Conclusion · Future work

From structured prediction to solver-ready assistance

The next step is to make learned structured predictions more scalable, more reliable, and more tightly integrated with exact optimization tools.

01

Better approximate inference

Explore stronger inference schemes, adaptive iteration budgets, or learned inference schedules that spend computation only where it is useful.

02

Scalable factor parameterizations

Design compact mappings for high-arity constraints that preserve useful structure without requiring full exponential-size factor tables.

03

Closer integration with exact solvers

Use learned marginals not only for standalone decoding, but also for fixing, branching, primal heuristics, or solver-side search guidance.

04

Broader BILP and MILP benchmarks

Test the approach on more diverse problem classes, larger instances, mixed variables, and benchmarks closer to practical optimization settings.

05

Feasibility and certificates

Strengthen the treatment of feasibility, repair, residual solving, and certification so predictions become safer to use inside optimization pipelines.

Perspective The long-term goal is not to replace exact optimization, but to develop learning components that expose useful structure to solvers.
This slide presents future work. The first direction is better approximate inference, including learned inference schedules or adaptive budgets. The second is more scalable factor parameterization, especially for high-arity constraints. The third is tighter integration with exact solvers, where learned marginals could guide fixing, branching, or primal heuristics. The fourth is evaluation on broader BILP and MILP benchmarks. The fifth is a stronger treatment of feasibility and certificates, so learned predictions can be used more safely in solver-assisted pipelines.
