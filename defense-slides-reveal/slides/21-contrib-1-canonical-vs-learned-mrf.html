At this point the canonical overcomplete parameterization has already been introduced in the MRF background section. This slide should therefore avoid re-deriving the canonical construction. The message is that the factor scopes are unchanged: they still come from the BILP variable-constraint graph. What changes is the source of the parameters. In the canonical MRF, parameters are fixed by the objective and by hand-coded feasibility penalties. In the learned MRF, the same parameter tables are predicted from GNN embeddings using variable and factor heads.
