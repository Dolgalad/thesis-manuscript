Diffusion-style training signal

Learn the inverse of a known corruption kernel

The forward process is fixed and stochastic: it samples a more corrupted state from \(q(x_{t+1}\mid x_t)\). The learned model approximates the reverse transition \(p_{\theta}(x_t\mid x_{t+1})\). .

MRF connection Each corrupted sample is partial information about the target: reverse denoising is conditional inference. Instead of correcting variables independently, an MRF layer can propagate constraint-aware information through the partially observed solution.
Present the forward process as a fixed Markov corruption kernel: from x_t we sample x_{t+1} according to q(x_{t+1} | x_t). This is not learned. It progressively removes or randomizes information until the state is close to noise. The reverse process is the learned direction: the model approximates p_theta(x_t | x_{t+1}) and reconstructs a cleaner solution one step at a time. This gives the bridge to MRFs: a corrupted solution is partial information, so reverse denoising can be viewed as conditional inference under structural constraints.
