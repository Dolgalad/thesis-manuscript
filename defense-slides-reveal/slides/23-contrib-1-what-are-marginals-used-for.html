Marginals guide construction, but do not directly guarantee feasibility.

The marginal of a variable is not just an independent probability output by a classifier. It is the result of running approximate inference on the MRF, so it contains information propagated through factors and neighboring variables. But marginals are soft local beliefs; they are not a binary assignment and they do not by themselves guarantee a feasible solution. This motivates a separate decoding step.
