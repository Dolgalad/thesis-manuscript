Takeaway: canonical MRFs encode the objective and constraints directly, but high-arity constraint factors require exponentially large assignment tables.
This slide moves the canonical parameterization to the MRF background. The left side recalls the overcomplete factor representation: each assignment of a factor has its own parameter. The right side specializes this to BILPs. Unary factors encode the linear objective coefficients. Constraint factors encode local feasibility by assigning zero to feasible assignments and a large negative penalty to infeasible assignments. This construction is fixed by the input problem and contrasts later with the learned MRF, where these tables are predicted from GNN embeddings.
