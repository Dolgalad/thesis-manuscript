1

Explicit output dependencies matter

Constraints induce dependencies between decision variables. Modeling these dependencies directly gives predictions that are better aligned with feasible combinatorial structure.

2

Approximate MRF inference gives stronger decoding signals

LBP and NMF transform local factor parameters into marginal beliefs, providing more informative scores than independent variable predictions.

3

Guided decimation benefits from conditioning

Recomputing beliefs after partial assignments makes decoding adaptive: each fixing changes the context for the remaining variables.

Takeaway The learned MRF is not only a predictor of variables; it is a structured source of decoding information that can guide both heuristic construction and solver-assisted fix-and-solve.
This is the closing slide for Contribution 1. The key message is that explicitly modeling dependencies between output variables matters for ILP solution prediction. Independent scores can be useful, but approximate inference over a learned MRF gives stronger decoding signals because it aggregates information through the factors. Guided decimation further benefits from conditioning: after some variables are fixed, beliefs are recomputed and the next decisions are made in a changed context. This prepares the transition toward the next contribution.
