Baseline idea: structured input, factorized prediction, feasibility recovered afterwards.
This slide describes the standard baseline pipeline used for BILP solution prediction. The instance is represented as a variable-constraint graph and processed by a GNN. The prediction head outputs one independent score per variable. These scores are then passed to a post-processing, correction, or repair step that constructs a feasible binary solution. The key limitation is that feasibility is not represented in the predictive distribution itself; it is imposed afterwards by the decoder.
