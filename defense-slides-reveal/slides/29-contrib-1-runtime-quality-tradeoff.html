Runtime versus solution quality

Structured inference improves decisions, but it is not free

Quality improves as more structured inference is used, but the gains eventually saturate while runtime continues to increase.

How to read the results

Inference is a controllable budget

1

Fast prediction

A direct GNN score is cheap and often useful, but the final scores are essentially one-shot variable preferences.

2

Structured marginal inference

LBP or NMF adds cost, but transforms local factor parameters into constraint-aware marginal beliefs.

3

Adaptive decoding

Guided decimation can improve decisions by conditioning on partial assignments, at the price of repeated inference.

Conclusion

MRF inference is most valuable when its structured signal changes decoding decisions enough to justify the added runtime.

The method exposes a tunable trade-off: more inference can improve quality, but the best operating point depends on the available runtime budget.
This slide should be presented as an honest synthesis of the previous results. The key message is not that MRF inference is always faster or always better. The point is that it provides a structured signal that improves decoding, especially under guided decimation, but repeated inference has a measurable computational cost. The inference budget and reinference frequency therefore become practical hyperparameters controlling the runtime-quality trade-off.
