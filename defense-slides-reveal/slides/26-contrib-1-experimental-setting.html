Runtime includes preprocessing, neural prediction, approximate inference, and decoding.

The experiments focus on Maximum Independent Set and Set Cover because they expose different structures. MIS has pairwise exclusion constraints, while Set Cover has coverage constraints that may involve many variables. The comparison includes factorized neural baselines, canonical MRFs with hand-designed feasibility-cost potentials, and learned MRFs whose factor potentials are predicted from the variable-constraint graph. The main metrics are solution quality, typically relative optimality gap, and end-to-end runtime.
