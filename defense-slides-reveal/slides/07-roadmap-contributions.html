This defense focuses mainly on Contributions 1 and 2: differentiable MRF inference and learned iterative refinement.
This slide gives the roadmap for the thesis contributions. The first contribution is differentiable MRF inference for binary ILPs, which is central to the thesis. The second contribution concerns learned refinement dynamics. The third contribution studies feature-to-factor mappings, which make the approach more scalable by mapping ILP features to MRF parameters. In the defense, I will mainly focus on Contributions 1 and 2, while Contribution 3 will be discussed more briefly.
