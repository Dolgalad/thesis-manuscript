Conclusion · Limitations

What are the boundaries of the approach?

The proposed models improve structured prediction and decoding, but they remain approximate tools for solver assistance rather than replacements for exact optimization.

01

Approximate inference is not a certificate

LBP and NMF provide useful marginal estimates, but they do not guarantee exact marginals, feasibility, or optimality of the decoded solution.

02

Repeated inference can be expensive

Guided decimation benefits from recomputing beliefs after conditioning, but this can dominate runtime when many inference calls are required.

03

High-arity constraints remain difficult

Large factors are hard to parameterize and infer over directly, motivating compact approximations and factor mappings with controlled expressiveness.

04

Some refinement results are exploratory

The refinement and fix-and-solve analyses indicate promising directions, but they should be viewed as preliminary evidence rather than a final solver integration.

Perspective These limitations point toward the same direction: structured learning is useful, but it must be paired with scalable inference and solver-aware evaluation.
