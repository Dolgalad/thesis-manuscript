From prediction to solver assistance

Use learned decoding to fix part of the problem

The learned model does not have to solve the full instance alone: it proposes confident fixings and lets the solver optimize the remaining degrees of freedom.

Evaluation protocol

Fix a fraction, then solve the residual problem

1

Choose a fixing ratio

Select a proportion p ∈ [0, 1] of variables to fix before calling the solver.

2

Unroll the decoder

Run the chosen decoding policy until approximately p · n variables have been fixed.

3

Solve the reduced instance

Keep the learned fixings as constraints and let SCIP complete the remaining unfixed variables.

4

Measure the full pipeline

Report both final solution quality and total time: prediction, decoding, fixing, and residual solving.

Reduced problem
fix x_i = \hat{x}_i for confident variables, solve over the remaining x_j.
Fix-and-solve connects structured prediction to practical solver assistance: the model reduces the search space, while the solver preserves optimization guarantees on the residual problem.
This slide introduces the fix-and-solve protocol before showing results. The goal is to make clear that learned predictions are not evaluated only as standalone heuristic solutions. Instead, the decoder is used to fix a controlled fraction p of variables. SCIP then solves the reduced problem over the remaining variables. This gives a practical interpretation of the learned marginals and scores: they provide confident fixings that can reduce the solver search space. The reported runtime should include the model forward pass, decoding or fixing time, and the residual SCIP solve.
