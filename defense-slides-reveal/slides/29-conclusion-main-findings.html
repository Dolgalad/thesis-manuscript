Conclusion · Main findings

Structured inference improves solution prediction

Across the thesis, the same pattern appears: learned scores are more useful when they are interpreted through the constraints of the optimization problem.

01

Output dependencies matter

BILP variables are coupled by constraints, so independent predictions miss important combinatorial structure.

02

MRF inference gives useful beliefs

Approximate inference turns learned local potentials into marginals informed by objectives and constraints.

03

Conditioning improves decoding

Guided decimation benefits from recomputing beliefs after partial assignments, making decisions adaptive.

04

Refinement is promising but problem-dependent

MRF-augmented refinement improves Set Cover results, while MIS highlights the need for careful decoding.

Takeaway The useful learned object is not just a score vector, but a structured signal that can guide decoding under constraints.
This slide replaces the longer conclusion sequence. The main findings are: output dependencies matter; MRF inference provides useful constraint-aware beliefs; guided decimation benefits from conditioning; and refinement is useful but remains dependent on the problem and decoder. The goal is to summarize the scientific contribution without spending too much time on limitations and future work.
