Canonical overcomplete exponential family
One feature per factor assignment
\[ \phi_{F,z}(x_F) = \mathbf{1}[x_F = z], \qquad z \in \{0,1\}^{|F|} \]
\[ \langle \theta_F, \phi_F(x_F) \rangle = \sum_{z \in \{0,1\}^{|F|}} \theta_{F,z} \mathbf{1}[x_F = z] \]
Limitation: a factor of arity \(d\) requires \(2^d\) parameters.
This slide introduces factor graphs as an explicit representation of the factorization used by the MRF. Variable nodes represent random variables, factor nodes represent local functions or energies. In the canonical overcomplete exponential-family parameterization, each possible assignment of a factor receives its own indicator feature and parameter. For binary variables, a factor involving d variables therefore requires 2 to the d parameters. This is expressive but becomes expensive for high-arity ILP constraints.
