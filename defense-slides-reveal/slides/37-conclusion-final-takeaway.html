Conclusion · Final takeaway

From independent variable scoring
to structured, constraint-aware prediction

Classical learning view Predict variables independently Useful scores, but weak representation of constraint-induced dependencies.
→
Thesis view Predict a structured model MRF factors and approximate inference put part of the ILP structure inside prediction.

MRFs provide a principled interface between machine learning and combinatorial optimization: learn local structure, infer global beliefs, and decode with constraints in mind.

01 Constraints shape the output space.
02 Approximate inference makes these dependencies usable.
03 Structured predictions can assist both decoding and exact solvers.
This is the final scientific takeaway. The thesis argues that learning for BILP solution prediction should not stop at independent variable scores. Since constraints couple the variables, predictions should also represent dependencies. Markov Random Fields give a principled way to encode objectives and constraints as factors, and approximate inference turns these factors into marginal beliefs that can guide decoding, guided decimation, and solver-assisted fixing.
